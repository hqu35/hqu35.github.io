MATH:234

The course is the epilogue in the standard calculus sequence, which focuses on multivariable calculus. We learned some basic differential geometry, partial derivatives, and finally double and triple integrals. Overall speaking, this course inherits the style in the calculus sequenece that only requires students to do basic calculation.

MATH:340

This course focuses on basic matrix algebra, with most of the proofs omitted. Just like how math 234 works,this course only requires students to do calculation instead of diving deep into the proofs of theorems. Though the disadvantage is pretty obvious: one might forget the knowledge pretty soon after the final due to the reason that no proofs are provided to really get you understand the logic behind.

Math 521

It's such a horrible experience when I took this course, the lecturer just copeid and pasted what's ever in her course notes, without providing any motivation nor explanation. But luckily, the exams were just asking us to reproduce the main theorems in the textbook. The course mostly covered chapter 1 to chapter 8 in Rudin's Principle of Mathemtical Analysis. We learned basic point set topology(which is not beginner friendly), continuous functions, differentiation Integration, Sequence and Series, Uniform Convergence, and some applications of these knowledge.

Math 541

I took the course with professor Milkahil Ivanov, who's strict but rigorous. We covered primarily group theory, and a bit of Ring theory. The first half of the course is relatively manageable. Everything becomes harsh and bad after the second midterm(Roughly speaking, quotient groups, orbits, direct products, etc). Overall speaking, the exams except the first midterm are hard, I did bad on the second midterm, but managed to get the second highest score on the final and the firts midterm.
