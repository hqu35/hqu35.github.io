MATH:234

This course is the last one in the standard calculus sequence, which focuses on multivariable calculus. I would say to a sdudent who's highly interested in pure math, this course is kinda not so useful. I should have skipped this course by taking the credit exam or otherwise take MATH 375 , but since MATH 375 is usually not offered in the spring term and I didn't self-learn multi-variable calculus during my gap period, this is my only option. We learned some very basic differential geometry, and then partial derivatives, and finally various kinds of integrals. Overall speaking, this course only requires you to memorize how to do basic calculations, but if you really want to dive deep into the proof of theorems, this course is not ideal.

MATH:340

This course focuses on basic matrix algebra, with most of the proofs omitted. Just like how math 234 works, I should have chosen Math 341 , which provides a proof-based glimpse into linear algebra, but due to an unsatisfied prerequisite (Math 234), this is again my only option. However, I would say that this course covers most key idea in linear Algebra I, though the disadvantage is pretty obvious: one might forget the knowledge very soon after the final given that no proofs are provided to really get you understand the logic behind.
